Secondary Playoff System

In Division I Collegiate Athletics, four different sports administer secondary tier tournaments. Their purposes are to provide opportunities for teams who missed the NCAA championship tournaments. Men’s and women’s basketball, women’s volleyball, and softball were the sports that issue secondary tournaments.

Administering secondary tournaments can work. Men’s and women’s basketball had success in fielding a secondary tournament. It was thanks to the NCAA that they made them happen. Triple Crown Sports was able to successfully revive the NIVC. The NIVC became a mainstay as a secondary tournament since its revival. However, we have to be wary of cases like the NISC. The NISC was a good idea on paper. However, it all came down to interest. Nearly no team was interested in participating in the NISC because they were not playing for a National Championship. It got to a point where the lack of interest could lead to the discontinuation of the NISC.

Administering a secondary tier tournament comes down to interest as well as the potential revenue. Would there be enough interest for teams to play in a secondary tournament if they do not make it to the FBS playoffs? In addition, would there be enough potential revenue to administer secondary playoffs? Because of the bowl games involved, the answer may be a resounding no. However, in my opinion, there could be at least enough interest and potential revenue to administer a secondary playoff while having bowl games.

A New Unified Secondary Playoff System

Note: Specific bowl game assignments throughout this proposal are illustrative and subject to change based on bowl game availability, sponsorship changes, and conference realignment. The structural framework of the playoff and secondary playoff systems remains valid regardless of which specific bowl games ultimately participate.

I would like to introduce my secondary playoff solution. The main secondary solution is going to be the National Playoff Invitational (NPI). The NPI would be similar to the current CFP. However, it would be a bit different. In addition to this, I am going to introduce another playoff to the secondary system called the College Playoff Crown (CPC). For each system, there would be four consolation bowl games for the eliminated first round participants and seven bowl games would rotate as quarterfinal, semifinal, and championship game. Four bowl games would rotate in a 4-year cycle to host the quarterfinals, semifinals and championship while two bowl games would rotate to host the semifinals, and one bowl game would function as a permanent quarterfinal game.

The NPI and CPC would re-purpose the bowl games to give them stakes, relevance, and guaranteed eyeballs that standalone bowl games increasingly can't attract. A bowl hosting an NPI quarterfinal, semifinal, or championship (or hosting a CPC quarterfinal, semifinal, or championship) has more value than a bowl hosting two 6-6 teams or a matchup of teams with losing records that nobody watches.

Why Introduce This Secondary Playoff System?

I wanted to introduce the Secondary Playoff System for the following reasons:

  • Provides meaningful postseason for teams just outside the CFP
  • Creates tiered championship opportunities (like basketball)
  • Maintains bowl game viability and revenue
  • Additional postseason opportunities for non-FBS power conferences

My proposed selection method for the 32-team field should resolve issues that would make the FBS championship playoff field unfair. Putting the best teams in the 32-team field should be fair for all FBS conferences’ apprehensions about less postseason opportunities. Introducing two Secondary Playoff System playoffs should further alleviate those apprehensions.

The primary 32-team playoff solution would have little to no impact on the implementation of the Secondary Playoff System. The only impact of adding the Secondary Playoff System is providing more postseason opportunities for FBS teams.

Selection Process

The selection process for the NPI and CPC are going to be different. Here is how each of them will happen.

NPI

The National Playoff Invitational (NPI) would operate similarly to the 2025 NIT. The first four teams out of the main CFP would be the top four seeds in the NPI. In addition, the next four out would be seeded 5-8. The eight seeded teams would host the first round, while the final three rounds would happen at bowl sites. Because all conference champions gain automatic bids to the playoff, the NPI would utilize the 5+11 format. The top five rated conferences concluding the regular season would get one automatic bid each to the NPI.

Basically, five automatic bids would be distributed based on the top five rated conferences concluding the regular season. The Conference Rank would be based on the average composite rankings of all the rankings from Massey Ratings. The rest of the playoff field would be filled with at-large selections.

CPC

The College Playoff Crown (CPC), on the other hand, would have no automatic qualifiers. They would accept teams that neither made the CFP nor the NPI.

Precedent for Multi-Tiered Postseason Systems

Some may express concern that a few bowl-eligible teams (6-6 or better) might not receive postseason opportunities under this system. However, multi-tiered postseason structures are normal across NCAA sports and don't diminish the value of championship access.

College Basketball's Four-Tournament Model: Men's basketball operates a successful multi-tiered postseason:

  • NCAA Tournament: 68 teams - the premier championship
  • NIT (National Invitation Tournament): 32 teams - historic secondary tournament
  • CBC (College Basketball Crown): 16 teams - competing as secondary tournament
  • CBI (College Basketball Invitational): 16 teams - third-tier opportunity

Combined, these four tournaments provide postseason opportunities for 132 of 362 Division I programs (36.5%). Notably, the NIT, CBC, and CBI does invite teams with losing records to fill their fields, which means teams with winning records are sometimes excluded while teams with losing records participate. Despite this, the multi-tiered system is widely considered successful—it provides meaningful championships at different levels without diluting the prestige of the NCAA Tournament.

Women's Volleyball Parallel: The sport successfully operates both the NCAA Championship and the NIVC (National Invitational Volleyball Championship), providing tiered postseason opportunities.

FBS Football Context: The proposed system mirrors basketball's proven model but with stricter standards:

  • CFP (College Football Playoff): 32 teams - premier championship
  • NPI (National Playoff Invitational): 16 teams - secondary playoff
  • CPC (College Playoff Crown): 16 teams - tertiary playoff
  • Traditional Bowl Games: 14 teams - standalone bowl games

This provides postseason opportunities for 78 of 136 FBS teams (57.3%)—significantly more generous than basketball's 36.5%. Critically, all three playoff tiers require at least a .500 record (6-6 or better), maintaining higher standards than basketball's secondary tournaments which accept teams with losing records. Having a few bowl-eligible teams miss postseason play is not a flaw—it's a natural consequence of field size limitations that exists across all NCAA sports, and the proposed system maintains more rigorous eligibility standards than basketball's widely-accepted model.

The Downsides

The biggest downside to implementing two second tier playoffs (or even one) is the potential of teams declining bids to participate. If a team misses a said playoff, they could see the Secondary Playoff System as meaningless. We’ve seen this happen in college basketball where teams declined bids to the NIT and College Basketball Crown. The teams that decline didn’t see any incentive for them to participate. In addition, those teams want to plan ahead of the transfer portal. The transfer portal currently starts the day after New Year’s (or January 3 if New Year’s is on a Sunday) and go until January 16th. In addition, the five-day window comes into play if a team’s final postseason game happens on or after January 12. Ideally, the transfer portal would happen after the CFP National Championship Game.

Another big downside is the investments and potential lack of interest. There needs to be enough interest from sponsors, TV Networks, teams, conferences, and more to administer Secondary Playoffs. We’ve seen the National Invitational Softball Championship not do well due to the lack of interest from teams. However, we have also saw how the NCAA and Triple Crown Sports have done well with operating secondary tournaments in basketball and volleyball. As long as there is a lot of interest, running two secondary tournaments wouldn’t be a problem.
