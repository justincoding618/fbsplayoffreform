Tertiary Playoff System

In Division I Collegiate Athletics, women's basketball is currently the only sport to administer a tertiary tier tournament. The purpose is to provide opportunities for teams who missed the NCAA Tournament and WBIT tournament.

Men's basketball also administered a tertiary tournament. The College Basketball Invitational (CBI) Tournament served as a tertiary tournament since 2008. The purpose is to provide opportunities for teams who missed the NCAA Tournament and NIT. However, the 2026 edition of the tournament was cancelled, leaving its future in doubt.

Once again, administering a tertiary tier tournament comes down to interest as well as the potential revenue. Would there be enough interest for teams to play in a tertiary tournament if they do not make it to the FBS playoffs nor the CPC? In addition, would there be enough potential revenue to administer tertiary playoffs? Because of the bowl games involved, the answer may be a resounding no. However, in my opinion once again, there could be at least enough interest and potential revenue to administer a tertiary playoff while having bowl games.

A Unified Tertiary Playoff System

Note: Specific bowl game assignments throughout this proposal are illustrative and subject to change based on bowl game availability, sponsorship changes, and conference realignment. The structural framework of the playoff and secondary playoff systems remains valid regardless of which specific bowl games ultimately participate.

System Overview

I would like to introduce my tertiary playoff solution. The main tertiary solution is going to be the Football Bowl Playoff (FBP). The Football Bowl Playoff would be a 16-team field. A total of 11 bowl games would be involved in the tertiary playoff system. Four bowl games would serve as consolation games for the eliminated first round participants. One bowl game would become an annual host to host the FBP Championship game, while the six bowl games would rotate to host the FBP quarterfinals and semifinals. Teams that make it to the College Playoff Invitational would still play in a bowl game even if they lose in the first round.

Top 7 Football Bowl Playoff Bowl Games

The top seven remaining bowl games in terms of payout and history would host the FBP Championship Game as well as the FBP quarterfinals and semifinals. One bowl game would become a permanent host for the FBP championship game, while 6 more bowl games would rotate as a FBP quarterfinal and semifinal host respectively.

Football Bowl Playoff Consolation Bowl Games

The next four bowl games in terms of payout and history would become consolation bowl games for FBP participants. These four bowl games would take teams eliminated in the first round of the FBP. Each consolation bowl game would pit any team eliminated in the FBP first round.

TicketCity became the first title sponsor, renaming the bowl game as TicketCity Bowl. After the first two editions, the bowl was renamed as Heart of Dallas Bowl. In 2018, the bowl game was renamed as the First Responsders Bowl. It was played around New Year's Day in the first four editions. Since 2014, it was played in late December. The First Responders Bowl was played in the Cotton Bowl Stadium from 2011-2018, and is played at Gerald Ford Stadium since 2019. The First Responders Bowl would serve as the FBP Championship Game.

Why Introduce This Tertiary Playoff System?

I wanted to introduce the Tertiary Playoff System for the same reasons as introducing the secondary playoff system. Once again, the primary 32-team playoff solution would have little to no impact on the implementation of the Tertiary Playoff System. The only impact of adding the Tertiary Playoff System is providing more postseason opportunities for FBS teams.

Selection Process and Playoff Structure

Here is how the Football Bowl Playoff Selection Process as well as the bracket structure is going to happen:

Selection Process

All of the selections will be at-large selections. The College Football Invitational would select the 16 best teams that were neither selected in the 32-team FBS playoff field nor the College Playoff Crown. Teams must have at least a 0.500 record or better to be considered for a FBP bid. However, if we come to a scenario that there are not enough teams with at least 0.500 records to participate, then the best teams that are one game below 0.500 would have consideration.

What If a Team Declines the FBP Bid?

If a team decides to decline a bid to play for the College Football Invitational, the bid would be awarded to the next best team. In addition, the team that decides to decline the FBP bid would end their football season and would not be eligible to play in a bowl game. Since a team that decides to decline would decline an at-large bid, the at-large bid would go to the next best team. There would not be a need to worry about teams declining automatic bids since all 16 FBP bids are at-large bids.

Playoff Structure

The Football Bowl Playoff would not seed the 16 teams that were selected. Home-field for first round would be based on the final regular season rankings based on the final regular season Massey Composite Ratings. Teams with a higher rating would host the first round of the FBP. The rest of the FBP would be contested at bowl game sites. The FBP Championship Game would be contested at the Poinsettia Bowl.

Precedent for Third Tier Postseason Systems

Men's basketball and women's basketball have held third tier tournaments. Those tournaments do not diminish the value of championship access.

Women's College Basketball

Women's basketball operates a successful multi-tiered postseason:

  • NCAA Tournament: 68 Teams - the premier championship
  • Women's Basketball Invitational Tournament: 32 Teams - recently established secondary tournament
  • Women's National Invitational Tournament: 48 Teams - recently downgraded as a tertiary tournament

These three tournaments combined provide postseason opportunities for 148 of 363 Division I programs (40.8%). Notably, the WBIT and WNIT does invite teams with losing records to fill their fields, which means teams with winning records are sometimes excluded while teams with losing records participate. Despite this, the multi-tiered system in Women's Basketball is also widely considered successful. Like Men's Basketball, Women's Basketball provides meaningful championships at different levels without diluting the prestige of the NCAA Tournament.

FBS Football Context

Once again, including the tertiary playoff system, the proposed system mirrors basketball's proven model but with stricter standards:

  • FBS Playoffs (College Football Playoff): 32 teams - premier championship
  • CPC (College Playoff Crown): 32 teams - secondary playoff
  • FBP (Football Bowl Playoff): 16 teams - tertiary playoff

This provides postseason opportunities for 80 of 138 FBS teams (58.0%), which is significantly more generous than women's basketball's 40.8%. Critically, all three playoff tiers require at least a .500 record (6-6 or better), maintaining higher standards than basketball's secondary tournaments which accept teams with losing records. Having 2-3 bowl-eligible teams miss postseason play is not a flaw. It's a natural consequence of field size limitations that exists across all NCAA sports, and the proposed system maintains more rigorous eligibility standards than men's and women's basketball's widely-accepted model.

How the Tertiary Playoff System Can Become Successful

The foundational success laid out in the secondary playoff system also applies to the tertiary playoff system. In summary, a caring organization, as well as no fees to participate, and NIL incentives is the best combination to make the tertiary playoff thrive. Like the secondary playoff system, the organization that is willing to sponsor the tertiary playoff system combined with the NIL financial incentives would allow the Football Bowl Playoff to thrive. Once again, not having both would likely result in the lack of incentives for teams to participate. Combining NIL incentives with the right organization that has the care and will to sponsor them would allow the Football Bowl Playoff to become a premier tertiary playoff field for teams that were not selected to play in the FBS playoffs nor the CPC.
