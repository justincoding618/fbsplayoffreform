Head Coaching Changes

Coaches leaving early is another pitfall to install an expanded playoff field. Even before the NCAA approved the Early Football Signing Period, some coaches have already decided to leave their current places and relocate to a “better” opportunity before the bowl games. When coaches do accept the opportunity in December, the recruiting dead period comes around and goes until January. In addition, the recruiting period prior to the Holiday dead period becomes an extremely stressful time for all coaching staffs involved to close out recruits and to get them to sign post-Thanksgiving, which would complete most or almost their recruiting class for the next season.

Currently, teams can call coaches and assistant coaches before or after the conclusion of the regular season and before the bowl games. Eventually, some will accept the position and leave their teams prior to their bowl game. Regardless of the proposed formats, having the 32-Team Playoff would completely bridge the gap between regular seasons and postseasons. It will also prevent coaches from agreeing to coach the new team too early.

Proposed Solution for Coaching Changes

What I think should happen for the FBS is to implement a similar coaching change rule in the NFL. The rules should be the following, regardless of the proposed FBS playoff format:

  1. If teams decide to fire their head coach before postseason begins, colleges/universities must fire him any time before or after the regular season concludes and no later than the start of the postseason, unless teams miss the postseason.
  2. Teams can interview any coaches who are not in any postseason, not in coaching, or have made the postseason. However, they must request permission from the college/university if a coach is under contract with that said team.
  3. If any teams' coaching staff loses within the playoffs, the coach and any assistant coaches can interview with any team that goes through the proper procedure to secure an interview. However, the interview must occur between when the team loses and when the bowl game kicks off.
  4. If any teams' coaching staff wins and advances to the second round, the team must require permission to interview the head coach and any assistant coaches from that said playoff team, which will have to happen before the second-round games kickoff.
  5. If any teams' coaching staff wins and advances beyond the second round, the team must require permission to interview the head coach and any assistant coaches from that said playoff team, which will have to transpire between the last and next postseason kickoff dates.
  6. Usually, the team will only allow interviewing the candidate once. However, if any teams' coaching staff wins advances to the National Championship Game, teams can schedule a 2nd interview, but must require permission from that championship-bound team. Once his team arrives at the national championship game site, teams will not be able to interview the candidate.
  7. If any coaching staff that are part of the playoff teams decide to accept new head coaching positions to any team, then that said coach can accept the offer, but must stay with his current team until their postseason run is over (this includes the season-ending bowl game).
