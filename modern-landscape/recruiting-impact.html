Recruiting Impact

The potential most frequent questions would ask the following:

  • Does this affect coaches and recruits arranging Fall off-campus visits?
  • How about post-playoff contact period in January and/or early February?

The NCAA recently approved an early signing period in football. Currently, the early signing period is a 3-day period, which begins on Wednesday before the conference championship games, as well as implementing a Summer early signing period so recruits can decide to either sign their NLI (National Letter of Intent) or wait until the current normal signing period. While players signing early sounds like a great idea, as mentioned earlier, it also encourages coaches to leave their teams much earlier before playing in their bowl game. In addition, coaches will have to balance bowl and/or playoff preparations and recruiting due to the early signing period. That is not an ideal situation for any involved teams, as teams will have to move quickly to find their successor and put together a good recruiting class.

The non-football sports do their signing period a bit differently. Division I basketball has a one-week early signing period that happens during the first two weeks of the season. Their regular signing period happens during the offseason and lasts only for four weeks. All other Division I Athletics have a 10-month signing period from November until August.

The NCAA made some recent changes to the FBS recruiting and transfer period. Firstly, they decided that from September up until November, there will only be a quiet period. Then, after the dead period on the week after Thanksgiving, there would be a two-week quiet period followed by a Holiday dead period. Having a quiet period only during the regular season definitely resolves the issues of stress of traveling to recruits’ hometowns while holding practices.

Key Recruiting Challenges

The 32-team playoff creates three primary recruiting challenges:

  • Early Signing Period Timing: Current early signing periods conflict with playoff preparation for 32 teams instead of just 12
  • Contact Period Conflicts: Coaches in deep playoff runs need time to recruit without juggling championship preparation
  • National Signing Day Flexibility: Ensuring adequate time for recruiting after the championship concludes

Each of these challenges has workable solutions that maintain recruiting fairness while accommodating an expanded playoff.

The Proposed Recruiting Solution

The ideal solution is to eliminate the early signing period entirely. The early signing period was introduced in 2017 to reduce recruiting pressure, but it has had unintended consequences:

  • Coaches leave for new jobs before bowl/playoff games to preserve recruiting classes
  • Players commit before seeing how coaching changes affect their fit
  • Teams in playoff contention face recruiting disadvantages vs. eliminated teams
  • High school players feel rushed to make life-changing decisions before seeing how their senior seasons conclude

Returning to a single signing period in February—after all bowl games and playoffs conclude—would resolve these issues. However, if the early signing period is retained due to pressure from coaches and programs, moving it to before Thanksgiving (as shown in the table below) minimizes conflicts with playoff preparation.

As you can see, since the regular season would end on the weekend before Thanksgiving, the early signing period would be moved up by one week. In addition, it would end one day before Thanksgiving. If there is no school on Wednesday before Thanksgiving, signing day would be done virtually.

Since there would only be two teams remaining after the semifinals, the contact period would be fine. Because the semifinals would happen on January 1 or 2 instead, many of the teams would not need to worry about juggling between contacting recruits and administering postseason practices. However, I think it would benefit pushing the Winter Contact Period down by one week (to also give National Champions and National Runner Up enough time to recruit). Conclusion

The 32-team playoff's impact on recruiting is manageable with thoughtful adjustments to existing calendars. By either eliminating the early signing period or moving it to before Thanksgiving, by shifting the contact period to after the national championship game, and by extending the National Signing Period slightly, we preserve fair recruiting access for all programs while accommodating an expanded playoff. The key principle is to make the recruiting periods follow the conclusion of postseason play, not compete with it. This ensures that coaches can focus on their teams during championship runs and that all programs, whether eliminated early or playing deep into January, have equal opportunities to build their recruiting classes. These adjustments require only minor calendar shifts from current practice, making them easily implementable alongside playoff expansion.
