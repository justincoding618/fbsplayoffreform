The 1991 season was the fourteenth season of Division I-A. Concluding the regular season, there was a split to who the #1 team was. Miami (FL) was ranked #1 in the AP Poll, while Washington was ranked #1 in the Coaches Poll. Instead of pitting both to play for a National Championship, both played in separate bowl games:

  • Miami (FL) faced off against Nebraska in the Orange Bowl. They shut out Nebraska to win the Orange Bowl.
  • Washington was obliged to play in the Rose Bowl due to their conference ties. They faced Big Ten Champion Michigan, and dominated the Wolverines in the Rose Bowl.

As both Miami (FL) and Washington won their bowl games, the polls produced a split National Championship for the second consecutive season. Miami (FL) won the AP National Championship, while Washington won the Coaches National Championship. This led to the creation of the Bowl Coalition in order to pit the top two teams in the same bowl game. In addition, this was the first season the Big East sponsored football.

It was also the first season the Big East sponsored football. The Big East playing football members consisted of Miami (FL), Syracuse, Pittsburgh, West Virginia, Rutgers, Virginia Tech, Temple, and Boston College. However, they did not crown a conference champion until the 1993 season. In addition, it was the final season Long Beach State played football before dropping the program.

During the 1991 season, the AP Poll was used to determine National Seeding as well as merit-based selection to determine the rest of the field. The playoff matchups are based on the final regular season AP Poll from 1991.

Conference Tie-Breakers

In the Big 8, Nebraska and Colorado both tied for first in the Big 8. Because they tied in their head-to-head matchup, it came down to the final rankings. Nebraska had the better rankings and overall record, which gave them the automatic bid.

The Big West had a tie for first between San Jose State and Fresno State. Fresno State would gain the automatic bid as they own the head-to-head tie-breaker over San Jose State.

The Big East sponsored football for the first time. However, they did not crown a champion this season, so teams that were considered for a bid only gained at-large bids.
