The 1995 season was the eighteenth season of Division I-A. It marked the first season of the Bowl Alliance, which replaced the Bowl Coalition. The Bowl Alliance included the Sugar Bowl, Orange Bowl, and Fiesta Bowl, while the Cotton Bowl lost its prestige. The Rose Bowl once again, was not included in the Bowl Alliance. The first season of Bowl Alliance almost had a major controversy. Had Ohio State finished an undefeated regular season, the Bowl Alliance would have received a major headache as a Big Ten and Pac-10 team cannot play the #1 or #2 team for the National Championship. Thanks to Michigan's upset over Ohio State, Northwestern completed their Cinderella season and earned a Rose Bowl invite and faced USC. For the National Championship, the Bowl Alliance successfully pitted Nebraska and Florida in the Fiesta Bowl to determine the National Champion.

At the end of the postseason, Nebraska was named the Consensus National Champion, while Florida finished as the #2 team in the country. I was able to compile the Bowl Alliance rankings by looking at the individual bowl games on Wikipedia.

The 1995 season marked the fourth season where the SEC held their conference championship game. In addition, it was the final season of the Big 8 and Southwest Conferences. The Southwest Conference would dissolve after the 1995 season, while the Big 8 would merge with four members (Texas, Texas A&M, Baylor, and Texas Tech) of the Southwest Conference to form the Big 12 Conference. In addition, it was also the final season for Pacific (CA) as they decided to discontinue their football program after the 1995 season.

In addition, Alabama and Miami (FL) were declared ineligible for the postsesason due to NCAA violations. While I was compiling the Bowl Alliance rankings, it was revealed that the Bowl Alliance rankings ranked ineligible teams as well (like its predecessor). Alabama and Miami (FL) would both rank in the Bowl Alliance Top 25 depsite being declared ineligible for the postsesason after Week 14 of the regular season.

During the 1995 season, the compiled Bowl Alliance rankings was used to determine National Seeding as well as merit-based selection to determine the rest of the field. The playoff matchups are based on the the final regular season compiled Bowl Alliance rankings from 1995.

Conference Tie-Breakers

The ACC has a tie for first between Florida State and Virginia. Virginia would earn the automatic bid as they own the head-to-head tie-breaker over Florida State.

The Big East has a tie for first between Virginia Tech and Miami (FL). Virginia Tech would earn the automatic bid due to the head-to-head tie-breaker over Miami (FL).

The Pac-10 has a tie for first between USC and Washington. However, in their only meeting, their match ended in a tie, so it came down to common opponents. The tie was broken based on the overall record. USC had the better overall record over Washington, so USC would get the automatic bid.

The WAC had a four-way tie for first place. Colorado State, BYU, Utah, and Air Force all finished with the same conference record at 6-2. Luckily for all four, they all played against each other, so the tie is broken through overall head-to-head record. Here is how all four teams fared:

Team Common Opponents
Colorado State 2-1 vs Air Force, BYU and Utah
Utah 2-1 vs Air Force, BYU and Colorado State
Air Force 1-2 vs BYU, Colorado State, and Utah
BYU 1-2 vs Air Force, Colorado State, and Utah

Air Force and BYU both were on the losing end as they went 1-2 against head-to-head common opponents. However, Air Force would finish 3rd as it owned the head-to-head tiebreaker over BYU. Colorado State and Utah went 2-1 against head-to-head common opponents. Colorado State would win the overall tie-breaker as it owned the head-to-head tie-breaker over Utah.
