The 1994 season was the seventeenth season of Division I-A. It marked the third and final season of the Bowl Coalition, which determined the National Champion. The Bowl Coalition included the major bowls (Sugar Bowl, Orange Bowl, Cotton Bowl, and Fiesta Bowl) except for the Rose Bowl, where they still have ties to the Pac-10 and Big Ten. The third Bowl Coalition saw a major controversy. Nebraska and Penn State were ranked as the consensus Top 2 teams. However, the Rose Bowl could not give up the Big Ten Champion as the Big Ten and Pac-10 champions were obliged to play in the Rose Bowl. Instead, the following happened:

  • Nebraska was matched up against Miami (FL) in the Orange Bowl. Nebraska went on to defeat Miami (FL) in a close match.
  • Penn State was pitted against Pac-10 Champion Oregon in the Rose Bowl. Penn State easily defeated Oregon to win the Rose Bowl.

At the end of the postseason, Nebraska was named the Consensus National Champion, while Penn State finished as the #2 team in the country. I was able to compile the Bowl Coalition rankings by looking at the individual bowl games on Wikipedia.

The 1994 season marked the second season the Big East determined the conference champion. For the SEC, it is the third season where they held their conference championship game. In addition, Memphis State rebranded as Memphis and were known as the Memphis Tigers since the 1994 season.

In addition, Auburn, Texas A&M, and Washington were ineligible for the postsesason due to NCAA violations. Once again, while I was compiling the Bowl Coalition rankings, it was revealed that that Bowl Coalition rankings ranked ineligible teams as well. This time, Texas A&M was #8 and Auburn was #9 respectively in the final regular season Bowl Coalition rankings depsite being ineligible.

During the 1994 season, the compiled Bowl Coalition rankings was used to determine National Seeding as well as merit-based selection to determine the rest of the field. The playoff matchups are based on the the final regular season compiled Bowl Coalition rankings from 1994.

Conference Tie-Breakers

The Big West had a three-way tie for first place. While UNLV played both Southwestern Louisiana (now known as Louisiana) and Nevada, Nevada and Southwestern Louisiana did not play against each other. Against common opponents, Nevada, UNLV, and Southwestern Louisiana against them were the following:

Nevada UNLV Southwestern Louisiana
W San Jose State (3-3) W Utah State (2-4) L San Jose State (3-3)
W San Jose State (3-3) W Utah State (2-4) W Utah State (2-4)
2-0 2-0 1-1

After common opponents, Southwestern Louisiana was eliminated after losing the tie-breaker. UNLV and Nevada were still tied after common opponents. UNLV owned the head-to-head tie-breaker over Nevada, so UNLV would earn the Big West's automatic bid.

Breaking the tie in the Southwest Conference was very tricky. Texas A&M finished first overall in the Southwest Conference. However, because Texas A&M was ineligible for the conference title that season, we move to the next best teams. Texas, Baylor, TCU, Texas Tech, and Rice finished in a 5-way tie! In head-to-head matchups, those five teams were still tied as all went 2-2 against each other. Breaking the tie against common opponents and conference strength didn't work either because all five teams played the same conference strength of schedule and their record against common opponents were tied. Since none of the five teams were ranked in the final Bowl Coalition ranking, the Southwest Conference broke the tie by determining the last trip to the Cotton Bowl. The automatic bid was given to Texas Tech as they never went to the Cotton Bowl in program history.
