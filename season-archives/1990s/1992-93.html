The 1992 season was the fifteenth season of Division I-A. It marked the first season of the Bowl Coalition, which determined the National Champion. The Bowl Coalition included the major bowls (Sugar Bowl, Orange Bowl, Cotton Bowl, and Fiesta Bowl) except for the Rose Bowl, where they still have ties to the Pac-10 and Big Ten. The first season of the Bowl Coalition was a success, as Miami (FL) and Alabama finished the regular season as the consensus top two teams. I was able to compile the Bowl Coalition rankings by looking at the individual bowl games on Wikipedia.

Alabama went on to win the Sugar Bowl and became the Consensus National Champion. The 1992 season also marked the season season the Big East sponsored football. However, once again, it did not crown a National Champion until the 1993 season.

In addition, this was the first season the SEC administered a conference championship game after adding Arkansas and South Carolina. Florida State joined the ACC and had success during its first season in the ACC. It was also the final season for Cal State Fullerton as they decided to discontinue their football program after the 1992 season. The Big West responded by adding Southwestern Louisiana (now Louisiana), Arkansas State, Northern Illinois, and Louisiana Tech for next season.

During the 1992 season, the compiled Bowl Coalition rankings was used to determine National Seeding as well as merit-based selection to determine the rest of the field. The playoff matchups are based on the final regular season compiled Bowl Coalition rankings from 1992.

Conference Tie-Breakers

In the Pac-10, Washington and Stanford tied for first place. Washington would get the automatic bid due to the head-to-head tiebreaker over Stanford. The WAC had a three-way tie for first place between Hawaii, BYU, and Fresno State. Hawaii was given the automatic bid due to the head-to-head sweep of BYU and Fresno State. BYU would finish second overall due to their head-to-head tiebreaker over Fresno State.

Last Teams In and First Teams Out

The last teams in and the first teams out would have been the following:

Last Teams In First Teams Out
Fresno State (8-4) Air Force (7-4)
Illinois (6-4-1) Kansas (7-4)
Mississippi State (7-4) Rutgers (7-4)
Wake Forest (7-4) Southern Miss (7-4)

With six conference champions ranked in the Top 25, there were three unranked conference champions. This left us with about four at-large bids for four unranked teams. The four at-large bids happen to be the last teams in.

Fresno State, Illinois, Mississippi State, and Wake Forest would all clinch the final playoff spots. Illinois' key win vs Ohio state and a tie vs Michigan placed them in a position to claim one of the final playoff spots despite bad losses to Minnesota, Iowa, and Northwestern. Despite a bad loss to South Carolina, Mississippi State clinched one of the final playoff spots with a key win vs Florida. Since the best teams remaining lack any quality wins, it came down to the overall body of work. Wake Forest was able to avoid any bad losses, which was enough to put them into the playoff field. Fresno State was able to pick up an 8-win season despite their bad loss to Oregon State, propelling them into one of the final playoff spots.

Air Force, Kansas, Rutgers, and Southern Miss were among the first teams out. Air Force picked up bad losses to Utah and Colorado State, which was enough to keep them out of the playoff field. Kansas's bad losses to California and Missouri were also enough to keep them out of the playoff field. Rutgers was the closest to a playoff spot, but after being edged out by Fresno State, they were among the first teams out. Southern Miss picked up bad losses to Northern Illinois and Auburn, and those kept them out of the playoff field.
