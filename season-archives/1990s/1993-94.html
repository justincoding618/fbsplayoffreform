The 1993 season was the sixteenth season of Division I-A. It marked the second season of the Bowl Coalition, which determined the National Champion. The Bowl Coalition included the major bowls (Sugar Bowl, Orange Bowl, Cotton Bowl, and Fiesta Bowl) except for the Rose Bowl, where they still have ties to the Pac-10 and Big Ten. The second season of the Bowl Coalition saw a split between who the #1 and #2 teams would have been. At the end of the regular season, Florida State topped the AP Poll despite their loss to Notre Dame, while Nebraska was the #2 team. In the Coaches Poll, Nebraska and West Virginia were the top two teams. I was able to compile the Bowl Coalition rankings by looking at the individual bowl games on Wikipedia.

According to the Bowl Coalition rankings, Nebraska was the #1 team, while Florida State was the #2 team, and both played in the Orange Bowl to play for the National Championship. Florida State narrowly prevailed to become the Consensus National Champion.

The 1993 season marked the first season the Big East determined the conference champion. For the SEC, it is the second season where they held their conference championship game. In addition, it was the first season Penn State competed in the Big Ten for football.

In addition, Auburn and Washington were both ineligible for the postsesason due to NCAA violations. While I was compiling the Bowl Coalition rankings, it was revealed that that Bowl Coalition rankings ranked ineligible teams as well. Auburn would rank as the #5 team in the final regular season Bowl Coalition rankings depsite being ineligible.

During the 1993 season, the compiled Bowl Coalition rankings was used to determine National Seeding as well as merit-based selection to determine the rest of the field. The playoff matchups are based on the the final regular season compiled Bowl Coalition rankings from 1993.

Conference Tie-Breakers

Wisconsin and Ohio State tied for first place in the Big Ten. However, in their only meeting, their match ended in a tie, so it came down to common opponents. The record against common opponents were the following:

6-0 5-1

Wisconsin went undefeated against the common opponents Ohio State played, while Ohio State's critical loss to Michigan denied them an outright Big Ten Championship. Because Wisconsin won the common opponent tie-breaker, Wisconsin would get the Big Ten's automatic bid.

The Big West had a two-way tie between Utah State and Southwestern Louisiana (which is now Louisiana). Utah State would win the Big West's automatic bid by winning the head-to-head tie-breaker over Southwestern Louisiana.

In the Pac-10, there was a three-way tie between Arizona, UCLA, and USC. UCLA would win the Pac-10's automatic bid due to the head-to-head sweep of Arizona and USC. Arizona would finish 2nd due to the head-to-head tiebreaker over USC.

The WAC had a three-way tie between Fresno State, Wyoming, and BYU. Fresno State played both BYU and Wyoming. However, Wyoming and BYU did not play against each other. Against common opponents, Fresno State, Wyoming, and BYU against them were the following:
