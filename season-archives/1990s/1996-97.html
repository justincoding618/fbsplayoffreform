The 1996 season was the ninteenth season of Division I-A. It marked the second season of the Bowl Alliance. The Bowl Alliance included the Sugar Bowl, Orange Bowl, and Fiesta Bowl. The Rose Bowl once again, was not included in the Bowl Alliance. The second season of Bowl Alliance this time had a major controversy. Florida State defeated Florida in the regular season to become the consensus #1 team. However, Texas upset Nebraska in the inaugural Big 12 Championship Game. Arizona State finished an undefeated regular season and were the consensus #2 team in the country. However, because the Big Ten and Pac-10 team cannot play the #1 or #2 team for the National Championship, the Bowl Alliance could not pit the top two teams to play for the National Championship. Instead, the following happened:

  • Florida was rematched against Florida State in the Sugar Bowl. The game was not close at all, as Florida dominated Florida State in the second half of the game.
  • Arizona State was matched up against Big Ten Champion Ohio State. Ohio State went on to defeat Arizona State on a last minute touchdown. Arizona State blocked the extra point, giving the Sun Devils a chance to tie. However, they ran out of time, giving Ohio State the victory.

At the end of the postseason, Florida was named the Consensus National Champion, while Ohio State finished as the #2 team in the country. I was able to compile the Bowl Alliance rankings by looking at the individual bowl games on Wikipedia.

The 1996 season marked the fifth season where the SEC held their conference championship game. In addition, it was the first season of the following conferences:

  • The Big 12 was formed from merging all Big 8 Conference members with four Southwest Conference members (Texas, Texas A&M, Baylor, and Texas Tech). The Big 12 split their teams into two six-team divisions and held a confernece championship game for the first time.
  • Conference USA was formed by merging two conferences that previously did not sponsor football (Metro and Great Midwest Conferences). Houston and five long-time Division I-A Independents, Cincinnati, Louisville, Memphis, Southern Miss, and Tulane joined that conference.
  • The WAC was formed by taking three former Southwest Members (SMU, Rice, TCU), two Big West members (UNLV and San Jose State), and long-time Division I-A Independent Tulsa. This led to the creation of the super conference, and the WAC held a conference championship game for the first time.

In addition, the 1996 season was the first season where overtime is administered in the regular season (overtime was first introduced in the 1995-96 bowl season). This meant that the game cannot end in a tie, and every game would end with a win or a loss.

During the 1996 season, the compiled Bowl Alliance rankings was used to determine National Seeding as well as merit-based selection to determine the rest of the field. The playoff matchups are based on the the final regular season compiled Bowl Alliance rankings from 1996.

Conference Tie-Breakers

In the Big East, there was a three-way tie between Virginia Tech, Miami (FL), and Syracuse. All three were tied head-to-head, while they were still tied between common opponents and conference strength of schedule. The tie-breaker came down to the final rankings, and Virginia Tech would earn the Big East's automatic bid.

The Big Ten had a two-way tie between Ohio State and Northwestern. Once again, Michigan defeating Ohio State denied Ohio State the outright Big Ten Championship. However, Northwestern and Ohio State did not play against each other. Let's see how they fared against common opponents:

Surprisingly, Northwestern and Ohio State played the same conference schedule, which means that they are tied in terms of common opponents and conference strength of schedule. The tie-breaker came down to the final rankings, and Ohio State would earn the Big Ten's automatic bid.

The Big West and C-USA had a two-way tie for first place. In the Big West, Nevada and Utah State shared the title. However, Nevada would earn the Big West's automatic bid due to the head-to-head tie-breaker over Utah State. In C-USA, Houston and Southern Miss shared the title. However, Houston would earn C-USA's automatic bid due to the head-to-head tie-breaker over Southern Miss.
