The 1997 season was the twentieth season of Division I-A. It marked the third and final season of the Bowl Alliance. The Bowl Alliance had a major controversy for the second consecutive season. Concluding the regular season, Michigan was the consensus #1 team, while Nebraska was the consensus #2 team in the country. However, because the Big Ten and Pac-10 team cannot play the #1 or #2 team for the National Championship, the Bowl Alliance could not pit the top two teams to play for the National Championship. Instead, the following happened:

  • Nebraska was matched up against SEC Champion Tennessee in the Orange Bowl. Nebraska would dominate Tennessee and easily win the Orange Bowl.
  • Michigan was obliged to play in the Rose Bowl. They faced Pac-10 Champion Washington State. Michigan won the Rose Bowl, but prevailed in a close game.

At the end of the postseason, the polls were split on a National Champion. Michigan won the AP National Championship, while Nebraska was able to overtake Michigan for the Coaches National Championship. The Bowl Alliance was replaced by the Bowl Championship Series, which included the Rose Bowl and allowed the most prestigous bowls to pit the top two teams to play for the National Championship.

The 1997 season marked the first season the MAC held their conference championship game after Northern Illinois re-joined the conference and Marshall moved up to Division I-A to join the MAC.

I was able to compile the Bowl Alliance rankings one more time by looking at the individual bowl games on Wikipedia. During the 1997 season, the compiled Bowl Alliance rankings was used to determine National Seeding as well as merit-based selection to determine the rest of the field. The playoff matchups are based on the the final regular season compiled Bowl Alliance rankings from 1997.

Conference Tie-Breakers

In the Big West, Utah State and Nevada were tied for first place. Utah State would get the automatic bid due to the head-to-head tiebreaker over Nevada. record. The Pac-10 also had a two-way tie for first between Washington State and UCLA. Washington State would earn the Pac-10's automatic bid due to the head-to-head tie-breaker over UCLA.
