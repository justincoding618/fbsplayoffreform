The 1990 season was the thirteenth season of Division I-A. Concluding the regular season, Colorado was the consensus #1 team while Georgia Tech was the consensus #2 team. Colorado was on a National Championship quest during that season, which included winning the controversial fifth down game vs Missouri. Georgia Tech and Colorado could have met to play for the National Championship. Instead, both played in separate bowl games:

  • Colorado faced off against Notre Dame in the Orange Bowl, which was a rematch of last year's Orange Bowl. This time, Colorado was able to outlast Notre Dame by one point.
  • Georgia Tech played in the Citrus Bowl vs Nebraska. Georgia Tech was able to eventually pull away from Nebraska.

Colorado and Georgia Tech both won their bowl games, so it came down to the polls to determine a National Champion. The championship was split. Colorado won the AP National Championship, while Georgia Tech won the Coaches National Championship. In addition, Houston, Oklahoma, and Florida were all ineligible for the postseason as they were on probation for violating the NCAA rules.

During the 1990 season, the AP Poll was used to determine National Seeding as well as merit-based selection to determine the rest of the field. The playoff matchups are based on the final regular season AP Poll from 1990.

Conference Tie-Breakers

In the MAC, Toledo and Central Michigan tied for first. Central Michigan would get the automatic bid due to the head-to-head tie-breaker over Toledo. Florida finished first place in the SEC. However, they were ineligible for the SEC Championship as they were on NCAA probation. The automatic bid went to the next best team, which was Tennessee.

The Big Ten had a four-way tie for first place. The tie came down to head-to-head. The tie-breaker was the following:

Team Common Opponents
Iowa 3-0 vs Michigan, Michigan State, and Illinois
Michigan State 1-2 vs Iowa, Michigan State, and Illinois
Michigan 1-2 vs Iowa, Michigan State, and Illinois
Illinois 1-2 vs Iowa, Michigan, and Michigan State

Iowa has the head-to-head tie-breaker sweep over Michigan, Michigan State, and Illinois, which means that Iowa would earn the Big Ten's automatic bid.

Last Teams In and First Teams Out

The last teams in and the first teams out would have been the following:

Last Teams In First Teams Out
Arizona (7-4) Baylor (6-4-1)
California (6-4-1) Colorado State (8-4)
North Carolina (6-4-1) Louisiana Tech (8-3)
Toledo (9-2) Wyoming (9-3)

With seven conference champions ranked in the Top 25, there were two unranked conference champions. This left us with about five at-large bids for five unranked teams. Oregon clinches one of the five at-large bids. Oregon's key win vs BYU and no bad losses were enough to put Oregon safely into the playoff field.

Arizona, California, North Carolina, and Toledo would clinch the final playoff spots. Arizona picked up key wins vs Illinois and USC, which was enough to overcome their bad loss to Stanford. California picked up key wins vs Oregon and Arizona, which were enough to overcome bad losses to Washington State and Stanford. North Carolina was the only team to give Georgia Tech a tie, which was just enough for them to clinch one of the final playoff spots. Since the best teams remaining lack any quality wins, it came down to the overall body of work. Toledo would gain the final playoff spot as they shared the MAC Champion with Central Michigan and did not lose too many games despite the lack of quality wins.

Baylor, Colorado State, Louisiana Tech, and Wyoming were among the first teams out. While Baylor was able to avoid bad losses, their tie vs Texas A&M was not enough to overcome their lack of quality wins. Colorado State's best win was vs Wyoming, but because Wyoming was among the first teams out, it was not enough for Colorado State to make the field. Speaking of Wyoming, losing the last three games of the season dented their hopes to make the playoff field. Louisiana Tech's schedule was too weak to be included into the field, with four of the teams played were against Division I-AA opponents.
