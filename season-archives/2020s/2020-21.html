The 2020 season was the seventh season of the 4-team CFP. It was an exception where a 4-team College Football Playoff would have been a wise choice due to the unforeseen circumstances. Alabama, Clemson, Ohio State, and Notre Dame were selected as the final four teams to play for the College Football Playoff National Championship. Under normal circumstances, this would be a 32-team field. However, given the pandemic's impact on scheduling and the uneven number of games played, a modified 16-team field would have been more appropriate for 2020, similar to how Division I FCS temporarily reduced their playoff from 24 to 16 teams. However, I will demonstrate both a 16-team field and a 32-team field. The 32-team format would use the selection method as normal. However, the 16-team format would use a customized selection method. Overall Conference Champions ranked in the Top 25 would earn an automatic bid into the playoff field. The rest would be filled with at-large bids.

The final CFP rankings was used to determine National Seeding, and then the rest of the CFP Rankings and merit-based selection were used to determine the rest of the field.

Key Notes Before Going Further

The 2020 FBS season was played during the COVID pandemic. Because of potential COVID outbreaks and concerns that happened, all teams played a different length of the regular season. In my project, the proposed regular season would run from early-mid August to Thanksgiving Weekend (for the Army-Navy standalone game). In addition, my normal playoff schedule would go from late-November to early-mid January. However, because the regular season ended on December 19, 2020 (due to COVID concerns), this meant the start of the playoffs was pushed back. Here is what would have happened to this playoff during a given COVID season.

Downsizing to 16 Teams

Due to the unusual circumstances around COVID, the playoff field would have the option to downsize temporarily to a 16-team field if necessary. In Division I FCS, nearly all the Division I FCS teams played in the spring. However, the Division I FCS playoffs was temporarily downsized from a 24-team field to a 16-team field. This was because some of the teams decided to opt out on either playing the remainder of the Spring Season or the entire Spring Season (since some teams also played in the Fall). Although the Ivy League did not send any team to the postseason at that time, none of their teams played in either the Fall or Spring seasons. This meant a smaller field to determine teams to play for a National Championship.

For Division I FBS, if there were teams that decided to opt out of playing in the postseason, this meant that we would have a smaller pool of teams that would be interested in playing in the postseason. A smaller pool of teams that would play in the postseason can potentially mean downsizing the playoff field so it can be conducive for that season. However, I also do not blame teams for deciding to opt out of playing due to COVID.

Keeping the Field at 32 Teams

Keeping the playoff field at 32 teams would be an option as well. Since nearly all the FBS teams played in the Fall season, there is definitely a good pool of teams that would be interested in playing in the postseason. The NCAA did waive bowl eligibility for the 2020 season only to allow as many student-athletes as possible the opportunity to participate in bowl games this year. In addition, we also have to be aware of COVID outbreaks in teams as well. This can happen to any team that would potentially participate in the postseason.

Even though there are going to be teams that decide to opt out of playing in the postseason, if the pool of willing teams is still sufficient for a 32-team field, then that would not be a problem. This will allow as many student-athletes as possible to potentially gain the opportunity to play for a National Championship. However, once again, I also do not blame teams for deciding to opt out of playing due to COVID.

Regarding COVID Outbreaks

The CFP made contingency plans in case of potential COVID-19 outbreaks. The contingency plan is holding the semifinals on Monday January 11, 2021 and Tuesday January 12, 2021, and then have the National Championship on Monday January 18, 2021. The 6-day rest for a team that plays on Tuesday is concerning. One team would have 7 days of rest, while another team would have only 6 days of rest before playing for a National Championship.

In addition, the NCAA held a basketball tournament in 2021 to determine a National Champion. VCU unfortunately had a COVID outbreak on their team. Because the NCAA could not postpone tournament games given the schedule, VCU unfortunately had to forfeit their game vs Oregon, and the NCAA declared it a no-contest. The rule during that time is that if a team had to withdraw within 48 hours of the tournament's commencement, the team would not be replaced. Instead, the opponent would "walk-over" to the next round. However, a team can be replaced in the tournament as long as it is more than 48 hours before the tournament commenced.

What happens if a team has an outbreak prior to the start of the playoffs? If this happens, then we can do the following:

  • If the team is not available to play due to a COVID outbreak or opts out, then a replacement team can be made no later than one week before the playoff begins
  • The start of the playoff would also have the option to be postponed by one week if any COVID outbreak happens with at least one playoff team
  • In an event that a game cannot be postponed, a team would "walk-over" when their assigned opponent cannot play due to experiencing an outbreak. The game would be declared a no-contest.

Playoff Management

For this scenario, each first round pairing and the corresponding quarterfinals would play on certain days in pairs. For example:

  • If the 5 seed plays their unseeded opponent on Tuesday, the 4 seed would also play their unseeded opponent on Tuesday. The winners of both games would play on the next Tuesday.
  • If the 7 seed plays their unseeded opponent on Wednesday, the 2 seed would also play their unseeded opponent on Wednesday. The winners of both games would play on the next Wednesday.
  • If the 6 seed plays their unseeded opponent on Thursday, the 3 seed would also play their unseeded opponent on Thursday. The winners of both games would play on the next Wednesday.
  • If the 8 seed plays their unseeded opponent on Friday, the 1 seed would also play their unseeded opponent on Friday. The winners of both games would play on the next Friday.
  • The winners of the quarterfinal games would have their semifinal game on either Thursday or Friday. The semifinal winners would then play for the National Championship on the following Saturday.

  • If the 5 seed plays their unseeded opponent on Tuesday, the 12 seed would also play their unseeded opponent on Tuesday. The 4 seed, 13 seed and their respective unseeded pairs would also play on the Tuesday. The winners of those games would play on the next Tuesday.

  • If the 6 seed plays their unseeded opponent on Thursday, the 11 seed would also play their unseeded opponent on Thursday. The 3 seed, 14 seed and their respective unseeded pairs would also play on Thursday. The winners of those games would play on the next Thursday.
  • If the 8 seed plays their unseeded opponent on Friday, the 9 seed would also play their unseeded opponent on Friday. The 1 seed, 16 seed and their respective unseeded pairs would also play on Friday. The winners of those games would play on the next Friday.
  • The winners of the second round games would have their quarterfinal game on either Friday or Saturday. The quarterfinal winners would then play in the semifinals on the following Saturday. Then, the semifinal winners would play for the National Championship on the following Saturday.
