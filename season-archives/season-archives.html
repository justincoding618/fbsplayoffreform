Season Archives

During the early days of Division I-A/FBS, National Champions were determined through the polls. In addition, most seasons did not pit the #1 and #2 teams in one bowl game. This resulted in controversies where organizers did not have a choice but to implement systems to determine a National Champion. However, all systems came with controversies.

After back-to-back split National Championships in the 1990 and 1991 seasons, the Bowl Coalition was the first attempt to pit the #1 and #2 teams into a bowl game. However, the system showed cracks in the 1994 season, where Penn State could not play Nebraska due to contractual ties to the Rose Bowl, which was not part of the Bowl Coalition. The Bowl Alliance replaced the Bowl Coaltion, but showed cracks again in the 1996 and 1997 seasons. Once again, like its predecessor, the Rose Bowl was not part of the Bowl Alliance. The 1996 and 1997 seasons saw the Pac-10 (1996 Arizona State) and Big Ten Champions (1997 Michigan) not being able to play the other top two team due to their contractual ties to the Rose Bowl.

After another split National Championship in the 1997 season, the BCS was introduced to truly pit the #1 and #2 teams in the country. It only worked in 6 out of the 16 seasons, which were 1998, 1999, 2002, 2005, 2012, and 2013.

The CFP replaced the BCS system since 2014. It is the current system to determine the National Champion. The CFP system also contained notable controversies like the previous systems. The 4-team system only worked in 3 out of the 10 seasons, which were 2015, 2019, and 2021. When the CFP expanded to 12 teams since 2024, it also showed cracks as well. So far, none of the seasons have worked out since the 12-team playoff was introduced.

We start with the 1978 season because this is where Division I was split into Division I-A and Division I-AA (which were later renamed as Division I FBS and Division I FCS). The 1998 season starts the modern era, where the BCS system was the first system to try to truly determine a National Champion. The 2020 season will still be looked; however, due to the ongoing pandemic that year, a 32-team playoff may not have been possible (and that would be the only exception). It could have been temporarily downsized to a 16-team field (like the Spring 2021 Division I FCS Postseason) if the FBS were pressed for time.
