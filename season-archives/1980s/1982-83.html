The 1982 season was the fifth season of Division I-A. Concluding the regular season, Georgia was the consensus #1 team while Penn State was the consensus #2 team. They both faced off in the Sugar Bowl, where Penn State emerged as the victors. They were named the Consensus National Champions, finishing #1 in both the AP and Coaches Poll. In addition, although Clemson won the ACC, they were ineligible to play in the postseason due to NCAA probation. If Clemson were allowed to play in the postseason, they would be in contention for a top 8 national seed.

During the 1982 season, the AP Poll was used to determine National Seeding as well as merit-based selection to determine the rest of the field. The playoff matchups are based on the final regular season AP Poll from 1982.

Notes

  • The Mid-American Conference (MAC) was relegated as a Division I-AA conference for just this season before being promoted back as a Division I-A conference the next season. Because of this, Bowling Green was the only Division I-AA (FCS) team to play in a bowl game and face off a Division I-A (FBS) opponent. If a playoff were to be held, Bowling Green would have likely been the only Division I-AA (FCS) team to play in the Division I-A Playoffs against a Division I-A (FBS) opponent.
  • Most of the Missouri Valley Conference members also reclassified to I-AA. Wichita State, Tulsa, and New Mexico State competed in Division I-A, while Drake, Illinois State, Indiana State, Southern Illinois, and West Texas State (now known as West Texas A&M) competed in Division I-AA.
  • All conferences have outright champions. However, Clemson was ineligible for the postseason as they were placed on probation by the NCAA. Maryland would earn the ACC's automatic bid as they were the next best team in the ACC after Clemson.

Last Teams In and First Teams Out

The last teams in and the first teams out would have been the following:

Last Teams In First Teams Out
Alabama (7-4) Boston College (8-2-1)
Arizona (6-4-1) Illinois (7-4)
Iowa (7-4) Miami (FL) (7-4)
North Carolina (7-4) Wichita State (8-3)

As the rankings only went up to a Top 20, with seven conference champions ranked in the Top 20, there were three unranked conference champions. This left us with about nine at-large bids for nine unranked teams. Florida State, Vanderbilt, New Mexico, Notre Dame, and Tennessee all safely made it into the playoff field. Florida State picked up key wins vs Ohio State without picking up bad losses. Vanderbilt picked up key wins vs Florida and Tennessee without picking up bad losses. New Mexico's only loss was to WAC Champion BYU. However, their 4th ranked offense in points/game jumps out despite their lack of quality wins. Notre Dame picked up key wins vs then #1 Pittsburgh and Big Ten Champion Michigan. Tennessee picked up a key win vs Alabama and tie vs LSU despite their bad losses to Duke and Georgia Tech.

Alabama, Arizona, Iowa, and North Carolina would clinch the final playoff spots. Alabama picked up key wins vs Penn State and Vanderbilt without picking up any losses. Arizona was able to pick up key wins vs Notre Dame and Arizona State depsite picking up a bad loss to Oregon. Iowa picked up a key win vs Arizona depsite picking up bad losses to Purdue and Iowa State. North Carolina clinched a spot by having a key win vs Vanderbilt while all their losses were within a possesion.

Boston College, Illinois, Miami (FL), and Wichita State were among the first teams out. Boston College picked up a key tie vs ACC Champion Clemson. However, their lack of quality wins prevented them from making the playoff field. Illinois' and Miami (FL)'s cases for playoff spots were denied due to their lack of quality wins. Wichita State was the closest to clinching a spot, but their schedule was too weak to be considered for a playoff spot. This would be the closest they would get as they would only win a total of 12 games through the next four seasons until dropping their program after the 1986 season. One more notable team that was left out of the playoff field was Southern Miss. While their win vs Alabama was great, it was not enough to overcome bad losses to Ole Miss and Division I-AA opponent, Louisiana Tech.
