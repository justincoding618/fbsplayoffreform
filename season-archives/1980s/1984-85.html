The 1984 season was the seventh season of Division I-A. The season was a notable season as BYU was the only undefeated team remaining. Concluding the regular season, BYU was the consensus #1 team. However, due to their conference ties, they were stuck playing in the Holiday Bowl against a mediocre opponent:

  • BYU faced Michigan in the Holiday Bowl. After a close match, BYU defeated Michigan to complete their undefeated season.
  • Oklahoma would face Washington in the Orange Bowl. Washington defeated Oklahoma and tried to claim their stake as a National Champion.

Concluding the bowl games, BYU was voted as the Consensus National Champion. Washington finished as the #2 team in the nation. Had Oklahoma won, some would have voted for Oklahoma as the National Champion. In addition, Florida had a spectacular season and won the SEC. However, they were banned from the postseason following an NCAA investigation. Clemson once again was ineligible to play in the postseason as well as the ACC Championship dues to probation imposed by the NCAA. In addition, Arizona was also ineligible to play in the postseason due to NCAA probation.

During the 1984 season, the AP Poll was used to determine National Seeding as well as merit-based selection to determine the rest of the field. The playoff matchups are based on the final regular season AP Poll from 1984.

Conference Tie-Breakers

In the Pacific Coast Athletic Association (PCAA), UNLV won the conference outright. However, it was revealed that UNLV played ineligible players on their team. Although they would still claim the conference's automatic bid at the time, it wasn't until March 1985 that the NCAA forced UNLV to forfeit all of their victories from not only the 1985 season, but the 1983 and 1984 season as well. Cal State Fullerton, the PCAA's runner-up, was retroactively rewarded the conference title. Despite the decision, UNLV would still be a representative for the PCAA's automatic bid.

In the SEC, Florida won the conference outright. However, it was revealed that Florida committed numerous recruiting and other rule violations. Their head coach was forced to resign, and after the investigation, Florida was not allowed to play in the postseason. This would mean that the automatic bid would go to LSU as they were the next best team in the SEC.

The Southwest Conference had a two-way tie between SMU and Houston. Houston was awarded the automatic bid due to the head-to-head tie-breaker over SMU.

Last Teams In and First Teams Out

The last teams in and the first teams out would have been the following:

Last Teams In First Teams Out

As the rankings only went up to a Top 20, with six conference champions ranked in the Top 20, there were four unranked conference champions. This left us with about eight at-large bids for eight unranked teams. Purdue, TCU, Cal State Fullerton, and Iowa all safely made the playoff field. Purdue picked up key wins vs Notre Dame and Big Ten Champion Ohio State without picking up any bad losses. Despite the bad loss to Texas A&M, TCU picked up key wins vs Arkansas and Southwest Conference Champion Houston. Cal State Fullerton may have been a surprise for at least some, but while they played one of the weakest schedules, their only loss was to PCAA Champion UNLV (who later forfeited all their games). Despite bad losses to Minnesota and Michigan State, Iowa picked up key wins vs Illinois and Purdue and a key tie to Wisconsin to put themselves into the playoff field.

Illinois, Rutgers, Virginia, and West Virginia would clinch the final playoff spots. Illinois picked up key wins vs Iowa and Wisconsin despite bad losses to Stanford and Michigan. Rutgers does have a key win vs West Virginia, and did just enough to overcome a bad loss to Penn State. Virginia's key win vs West Virginia was enough to place them into the playoff field. West Virginia picked up a key win vs Boston College, which was was enough to place them into the playoff field.

Air Force, Georgia, Georgia Tech, and Tennessee were among the first teams out. Air Force was able to pick up a key win vs Notre Dame, but it was not enough to overcome bad losses to Utah and Wyoming. Georgia was on their way to a playoff spot. However, they lost the last three game to close out the regular season. This has led them to landing on the wrong side of the fringe. Georgia Tech did pick up a win vs Georgia, but their win vs Alabama declined over the course of the regular season. While their tie vs Virginia does stand out, it was not enough to put them into the playoff field. Tenneesse does have a win over Georgia Tech, but it was not enough to overcome their 0-2 record vs playoff-bound teams.
