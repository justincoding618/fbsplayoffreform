The 1989 season was the twelfth season of Division I-A. It was also the first season where the AP and Coaches Poll extended the rankings to a Top 25. Concluding the regular season, Colorado was the consensus #1 team while Miami (FL) was the consensus #2 team after upsetting Notre Dame late in the regular season. As a result of Miami (FL)'s upset of Notre Dame, Colorado and Miami (FL) played in separate bowl games:

  • Colorado faced off against Notre Dame in the Orange Bowl. Notre Dame would handle Colorado easily.
  • Miami (FL) was matched up against Alabama in the Sugar Bowl. Miami (FL) would go on to defeat Alabama by eight points.

With Colorado losing in the Orange Bowl, it came down to the polls to determine a National Champion. The polls determined Miami (FL) as the National Champion, with #2 being split between Notre Dame and Florida State. Notre Dame was ranked #2 in the AP Poll, while Florida State finished #2 in the Coaches Poll. In addition, Houston was ineligible for the postseason as they violated the NCAA rules and were on probation.

During the 1989 season, the AP Poll was used to determine National Seeding as well as merit-based selection to determine the rest of the field. The playoff matchups are based on the final regular season AP Poll from 1989.

Conference Tie-Breakers

In the ACC, Virginia and Duke tied for first place in the ACC. However, Virginia would win the automatic bid due to their head-to-head tiebreaker over Duke.

Since 1989, the rankings have expanded to a Top 25. With seven conference champions ranked in the Top 25, there were two unranked conference champions. This left us with about five at-large bids for five unranked teams. Washington clinches one of the five at-large bids. Washington's key wins over Oregon and Texas A&M while avoiding bad losses safely puts Washington into the playoff field.

Arizona, Air Force, Oregon, and Virginia Tech would clinch the final playoff spots. Arizona was able to pick up a key win vs Washington. This was enough to overcome their bad loss to California and clinch one of the final playoff spots. Air Force was able to gain a key tie to Hawaii, which was enough for them to clinch one of the final playoff spots. Oregon picked up a key win vs Arizona and that was enough to overcome bad losses to Washington State and Stanford. Virignia Tech was able to pick up a key win vs West Virginia to overcome their bad loss to East Carolina.

Arizona State, Florida, Northern Illinois, and Ole Miss were among the first teams out. Arizona State was able to pick up a key win vs Washington, but it was not enough to overcome a bad tie to Oregon State and a bad loss to UCLA. Florida just did not have the quality wins to overcome their quality losses. Northern Illinois was able to do well as an Independent, but lack the quality wins to be considered into the playoff field. Ole Miss was able to pick up a win vs Florida, but a bad loss to LSU plus the lack of quality wins kept them out of a playoff spot.
