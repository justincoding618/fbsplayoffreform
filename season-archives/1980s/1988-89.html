The 1988 season was the eleventh season of Division I-A. Concluding the regular season, Notre Dame was the consensus #1 team, while Miami (FL) was the consensus #2 team. However, both played in separate bowl games:

  • Notre Dame and West Virginia played in the Fiesta Bowl, which decided the National Championship.
  • Miami (FL) instead played in the Orange Bowl against Big 8 Champion Nebraska.
  • If West Virginia were to win the Fiesta Bowl, there was a possibility of a split National Championship.

Notre Dame emerged victorious against West Virginia in the Fiesta Bowl, becoming the Consensus National Champion. Miami (FL) won the Orange Bowl, finishing #2 in the final rankings. In addition, it was also the first season of the Pacific Coast Athletic Association (PCAA) rebranding as the Big West Conference. SMU also served their second year of their death penalty before rebooting their college football program.

During the 1988 season, the AP Poll was used to determine National Seeding as well as merit-based selection to determine the rest of the field. The playoff matchups are based on the final regular season AP Poll from 1988.

Last Teams In and First Teams Out

The last teams in and the first teams out would have been the following:

Last Teams In First Teams Out
Arizona (7-4) Hawaii (9-3)
BYU (8-4) Indiana (7-3-1)
Louisville (8-3) Iowa (6-3-3)
Southern Miss (9-2) Wake Forest (6-4-1)

As the rankings only went up to a Top 20, with seven conference champions ranked in the Top 20, there were two unranked conference champions. This left us with about ten at-large bids for ten unranked teams.

Duke, South Carolina, NC State, UTEP, Colorado, and Virginia all safely made the playoff field. Despite a bad loss to Maryland, Duke was able to pick up a win vs Virginia and tie vs NC State to keep them safe into the field. South Carolina was able to pick up key wins vs Georgia and NC State to be safely into the field. Despite a bad loss to Maryland, NC State was able to pick up a key win vs ACC Champion Clemson as well as tie vs Duke to safely be in the field. UTEP's overall record was enough to safely make it into the field desptie a weak schedule. Colorado's win vs Big West Champion Fresno State and their avoidance of bad losses were enough to safely put them into the playoff field. Virginia was able pick up a key win vs NC State and avoid bad losses to keep them safely into the playoff field.

Arizona, BYU, Louisville, and Southern Miss would clinch the final playoff spots. Arizona's win vs Washington State was enough to overcome their bad loss to California and put them into the playoff field. BYU picked up a key win vs UTEP to propel them into one of the final playoff spots. Louisville's key win vs Virginia was enough for them to make the playoff field despite a bad loss to Maryland. Southern Miss's win vs Louisville and their avoidance of bad losses were enough to put them into the playoff field.

Hawaii, Indiana, Iowa, and Wake Forest were among the first teams out. Hawaii was able to avoid picking up bad losses, but their lack of quality wins prevented them from making the playoff field. Indiana and Iowa were plagued by a weakened Big Ten, which kept both of them out of the playoff field. Wake Forest picked up a key win vs Duke, but their four losses and a tie kept them out of the playoff field.
