The 1987 season was the tenth season of Division I-A. Concluding the regular season, Oklahoma was the consensus #1 team while Miami (FL) was the consensus #2 team. For the second consecutive season, both were able to play against each other, this time in the Orange Bowl, where the winner of the game would be declared the National Champion. Miami (FL) defeated Oklahoma in a very close match, becoming the Consensus National Champion. Florida State would finish as the #2 team, with their only loss against Miami (FL). Oklahoma would finish as the consensus #3 team.

In addition, this is also the first year out of two years of SMU's death penalty. They were issued a two-year death penalty due to their illegal slush fund to pay recruits and players plus additional other violations.

During the 1987 season, the AP Poll was used to determine National Seeding as well as merit-based selection to determine the rest of the field. The playoff matchups are based on the final regular season AP Poll from 1987.

Last Teams In and First Teams Out

The last teams in and the first teams out would have been the following:

Last Teams In First Teams Out
Air Force (9-3) Arizona State (6-4-1)

Texas Tech (6-4-1) Washington (6-4-1)

As the rankings only went up to a Top 20, with six conference champions ranked in the Top 20, there were three unranked conference champions. This left us with about nine at-large bids for nine unranked teams. Indiana, BYU, Arkansas, Virginia, and Alabama all safely made the playoff field. Indiana picked up wins vs Michigan and Ohio State, which were enough to overcome a bad loss to Kentucky. Despite a bad loss to TCU, BYU's win vs Air Force propelled them safely into the playoff field. Alabama was able to pick up key wins vs Penn State, Tennessee, and LSU and overcome their bad losses to Memphis State and Florida. Virginia and Arkansas both did enough to avoid bad losses.

Air Force, Michigan, Ohio State, and Texas Tech would clinch the final playoff spots. Air Force also did just enough to avoid bad losses. Michigan picked up a key win vs Iowa, which was enough to put them into the playoff field. Ohio State was on the verge of missing the playoff field. However, thanks to their win vs Michigan in the regular season finale, they did just enough to clinch one of the final playoff spots. Texas Tech's win vs Southwest Conference Champion Texas A&M was enough to propel them to one of the final playoff spots.

Arizona State, Colorado, UTEP, and Washington were among the first teams out. Arizona State's best win was vs UTEP, but it was not enough to propel them into the field. Colorado picked up a bad loss to Oregon, and their regular-season finale loss to Nebraska pushed them out of a playoff spot. UTEP also did not have any bad losses, and the last two losses pushed them out of a playoff spot. Washington had a win vs Arizona State, but it was not enough as their bad loss to Oregon and bad tie to Arizona denied them a playoff spot.
