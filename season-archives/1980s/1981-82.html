The 1981 season was the fourth season of Division I-A. Concluding the regular season, Clemson was the consensus #1 team. They won the Orange Bowl and was named the Consensus National Champion, finishing #1 in both the AP and Coaches Poll. In addition, although SMU won the Southwest Conference, they were ineligible to play in the postseason due to NCAA probation. Along with SMU, Miami (FL) and Arizona State were also ineligible for the postseason because both were on probation by the NCAA. Those three schools would have been in contention for playoff bids.

During the 1981 season, the AP Poll was used to determine National Seeding as well as merit-based selection to determine the rest of the field. The playoff matchups are based on the final regular season AP Poll from 1981.

Notes

  • The Ivy League did play football during the season. However, no teams from the Ivy League would be able to participate because the Ivy League did not allow teams to participate in the postseason at the time.
  • The 1981 season marked the final season where the Ivy League, Southern Conference, and Southland Conference competed in Division I-A. Those three conferences were relegated and competed in Division I-AA (now known as Division I FCS) since 1982.
  • This would have been UT-Arlington's only appearance in the Division I-A playoff field before dropping their football program after completing just their fourth I-AA season in 1985.

Conference Tie-Breakers

In the Southwest Conference, SMU won it outright. However, because SMU was ineligible for the postseason, the automatic bid in the Southwest Conference went to the next best team. The Southwest Conference's automatic bid went to Texas.

The Big Ten, Missouri Valley, and SEC had co-champions. In the Missouri Valley Conference, Tulsa would earn the conference's automatic bid due to the head-to-head tie-breaker over Drake. In the Big Ten, Ohio State and Iowa both shared the conference title. Comparing their conference schedules, both Ohio State and Iowa played the same conference opponents, but did not play against each other. Therefore, both had the same common opponents and conference strength of schedule. The tie-breaker was decided through the final regular season rankings. Iowa would earn the conference's automatic bid. Alabama and Georgia both finished undefeated conference seasons. The tie-breaker came down to the conference strength. This is what it looked like for each:
