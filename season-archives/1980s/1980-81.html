The 1980 season was the third season of Division I-A. Concluding the regular season, Georgia was the consensus #1 team. They won the Sugar Bowl and was named the Consensus National Champion, finishing #1 in both the AP and Coaches Poll.

During the 1980 season, the AP Poll was used to determine National Seeding as well as merit-based selection to determine the rest of the field. The playoff matchups are based on the final regular season AP Poll from 1980.

Last Teams In and First Teams Out

The last teams in and the first teams out would have been the following:

Last Teams In First Teams Out
Arizona State (8-3) Maryland (8-3)
Oregon (6-3-2) Missouri (8-3)
Southern Miss (8-3) Tennessee State (9-1)
Texas (7-4) Virginia Tech (8-3)

As the rankings only went up to a Top 20, with seven conference champions ranked in the Top 20, there were five unranked conference champions. This left us with about seven at-large bids for seven unranked teams. Florida, LSU, and Navy are all safely into the playoff field. While two of Navy's three losses were bad, they picked up a key win vs Pac-10 Champion Washington. Florida was able to pick up a win vs Mississippi State without picking up bad losses. Although LSU picked up a bad loss to Rice, LSU's win vs Florida also placed them safely into the field.

Arizona State, Texas, Oregon, and Southern Miss were the final teams to clinch at-large spots. Texas was able to pick up key wins vs Houston and Oklahoma and was enough to overcome their bad losses to Texas Tech and Texas A&M. Arizona State picked up a key win vs Oregon without picking up any bad losses. Speaking of Oregon, although their overall record looks polarizing, they picked up key wins vs UCLA and Pac-10 Champion Washington while also picking up a key tie to USC. Despite picking up bad losses to Auburn and Lousiville, Southern Miss was able to clinch the final playoff spot with their win vs Mississippi State.

Maryland unfortunately lacked the quality wins they needed to back up their overall record. While their losses are not bad, they did not have any key wins on their resume. Missouri also fell on the wrong side of the fringe due to the lack of quality wins. Tennessee State only lost one game in the regular season. However, their schedule and resume was far too weak as the majority of their schedule were Division I-AA opponents plus a Division II opponent. While their schedule is stronger than Tennessee State's, Virginia Tech also fell on the wrong side of the fringe as their resume lacked quality wins as well.
