The 1985 season was the eighth season of Division I-A. Concluding the regular season, Penn State was the consensus #1 team. Miami (FL) was #2 in the AP Poll, while Oklahoma was #2 in the Coaches Poll, but is not too far behind in the AP Poll at #3. Penn State accepted the Orange Bowl invite to play against Oklahoma:

  • Penn State faced off against Oklahoma in the Orange Bowl. Oklahoma went on to put Penn State away.
  • Miami (FL) would face Tennessee in the Sugar Bowl. However, Tennesssee completely dominated Miami (FL), outscoring the Hurricanes 35-7.

With Penn State and Miami (FL) both losing in the bowl games, the polls determined Oklahoma as the Consensus National Champion. Michigan would finish #2 and Penn State would drop to #3. In addition, Florida finished tied for first in the SEC. However, they were ineligible to play in the postseason and for the SEC Championship due to NCAA probation.

During the 1985 season, the AP Poll was used to determine National Seeding as well as merit-based selection to determine the rest of the field. The playoff matchups are based on the final regular season AP Poll from 1985.

Conference Tie-Breakers

Nearly all the conferences have outright champions. However, the WAC title was shared between BYU and Air Force. BYU would get the WAC's automatic bid due to the head-to-head tie-breaker over Air Force. In addition, the SEC had a two-way tie for first place. However, because Florida was ineligible for the SEC Championship nor postseason, the SEC champion went to Tennessee despite losing the head-to-head tie-breaker to Florida.

Last Teams In and First Teams Out

The last teams in and the first teams out would have been the following:

Last Teams In First Teams Out
Arizona (8-3) Army (8-3)
Colorado (7-4) Syracuse (7-4)
Illinois (6-4-1) Utah (8-4)
Miami (OH) (8-2-1) West Virginia (7-3-1)

As the rankings only went up to a Top 20, with eight conference champions ranked in the Top 20, there were two unranked conference champions. This left us with about ten at-large bids for ten unranked teams.

Texas, Georgia, Baylor, Arizona, Georgia Tech, and Michigan State were among the teams that safely made the playoff field. Texas picked up key wins vs Arkansas and Baylor, which was enough to overcome their bad loss to SMU. Georgia picked up key wins vs Baylor and Florida and overcame their bad tie to Vanderbilt. Baylor picked up a key win vs Southwest Conference Champion Texas A&M without picking up bad losses. Georgia Tech was able to pick up a key win vs Georgia to end the regular season and bolster their playoff case. Both Arizona and Michigan State were able to pick up a win vs Arizona State, which were enough to safely put them into the playoff field.

Arizona State, Colorado, Illinois, and Miami (OH) would clinch the last playoff spots. Colorado picked up a key win vs Arizona and did enough to avoid bad losses. Arizona State and Miami (OH) did not have any quality wins. However, they both did just enough by avoiding bad losses. Illinois had the most polarizing resume, but their win vs Ohio State and tie vs Michigan were enough to give them the final playoff spot.

Army, Syracuse, Utah, and West Virginia were among the first teams out. Army's schedule was too weak to be included in the field as four of their games were against Division I-AA opponents. Syracuse's bad loss to Mississippi State was costly as losing to West Virginia knocked them out of a playoff spot. Speaking of West Virginia, their bad loss to Virginia as well as a bad tie to Pittsburgh kept them out of the playoff field. Utah had a legitimate case, but their bad loss to Colorado State near the end of the regular season costed them a playoff spot.
