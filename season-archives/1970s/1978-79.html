The 1978 season was the first season of Division I-A. Concluding the regular season, Penn State was the consensus #1 team in the nation. They accepted a bid to the Sugar Bowl to face Alabama. However, after Alabama defeated Penn State in the Sugar Bowl, the polls decided the National Champion.

  • Alabama was named the AP National Champion after defeating Penn State in the Sugar Bowl.
  • USC topped the Coaches Poll after defeating Michigan in the Rose Bowl. They finished as the #2 team in the AP Poll despite defeating Alabama earlier in the regular season.

In addition, despite sharing the Big Ten championship, Michigan State was ineligible for the postseason due to being placed on probation by the NCAA.

During the 1978 season, the AP Poll was used to determine National Seeding as well as merit-based selection to determine the rest of the field. The playoff matchups are based on the final regular season AP Poll from 1978.

Conference Tie-Breakers

Here, we had to break ties in the Big 8, Big Ten, Pacific Coast Athletic Association (PCAA), Southern, and Southland Conferences. In the Big 8, Nebraska and Oklahoma shared the conference title. However, Nebraska would get the automatic bid due to the head-to-head tiebreaker over Oklahoms. The Big Ten was shared by Michigan and Michigan State. However, because Michigan State was on probation and was ineligible for the postseason, Michigan was given the automatic bid by default despite losing the head-to-head tie-breaker to Michigan State. Utah State would get the PCAA's automatic bid due to the head-to-head tie-breaker over San Jose State. Chattanooga would get the Southern Conference's automatic bid due to the head-to-head tie-breaker over Furman. Finally, Louisiana Tech would get the Southland Conference's automatic bid due to the head-to-head tie-breaker over Arkansas State.
