The 1979 season was the second season of Division I-A. Concluding the regular season, the polls were split on the #1 team. The AP Poll considered Ohio State as the #1 team in the nation. However, the coaches poll considered Alabama as the #1 team, while Ohio State was #3. Ohio State had a chance to at least clinch the AP National Championship, but fell to USC by one point in the Rose Bowl. Alabama, on the other hand, defeated Arkansas in the Sugar Bowl, and was the Consensus National Champion, finishing #1 in both the AP and Coaches Poll.

During the 1979 season, the AP Poll was used to determine National Seeding as well as merit-based selection to determine the rest of the field. The playoff matchups are based on the final regular season AP Poll from 1979.

Notes

  • The Ivy League did play football during the season. However, no teams from the Ivy League would be able to participate because the Ivy League did not allow teams to participate in the postseason at the time.
  • West Texas State is now known as West Texas A&M and plays football at Division II since 1986.

Conference Tie-Breakers

All conferences except for the Southwest Conference have outright champions. The Southwest Conference has a tie between Houston and Arkansas. Houston would earn the Southwest Conference's automatic bid due to the head-to-head tiebreaker over Arkansas.
