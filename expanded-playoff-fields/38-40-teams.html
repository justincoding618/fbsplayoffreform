38, 39, or 40-Team Playoff Field

Expanding to either a 38, 39, or 40-team playoff field would be an option if Division I FBS and Division I FCS remain intact. Along with the main 32-team playoff field, there would be play-in games for Division I FBS teams to play their way into the main 32-team playoff field. The top Division I FBS teams (including conference champions) clinch a playoff spot outright, while the best remaining Division I FBS teams that did just enough to be considered for the main playoff field would participate in the play-in games (including the lower ranked conference champions). Division I FBS teams that make it to the play-in games must survive the Play-In Series to earn their spot in the main 32-team playoff field. The Play-In Series games can be hosted either at campus sites (like the first two rounds of the main 32-team playoff), which keeps the format consistent and gives those play-in teams home-field advantage if they earned it through seeding, or through bowl games at pre-determined neutral sites. For bowl games, they would become the Wildcard Bowl Series. The higher ranked team would be the designated home team and the winner of any of the Wildcard Bowl Games would advance to the 32-team playoff field.

The Options

Here is how the playoff can be expanded to a 6th round if Division I FBS stays intact:

38-Team Playoff Field

  • 6 play-in games to get to 32
  • Teams ranked in the final CFP Top 25 plus the top unranked CFP team clinch a guaranteed spot in the main 32-team field
  • Unranked CFP Teams that gained enough consideration to be in the main 32-team field must survive the Play-In Series (including unranked Conference Champions)

39-Team Playoff Field

  • 7 play-in games to get to 32
  • Teams ranked in the final CFP Top 25 clinch a guaranteed spot in the main 32-team field
  • Unranked CFP Teams that gained enough consideration to be in the main 32-team field must survive the Play-In Series (including unranked Conference Champions)

40-Team Playoff Field

  • 8 play-in games to get to 32
  • Teams ranked in the final CFP Top 20 clinch a guaranteed spot in the main 32-team field; last four byes become highly competitive
  • The #25 Ranked CFP Team and any unranked CFP team that gained enough consideration to be in the main 32-team field must survive the Play-In Series (including unranked Conference Champions)

For either scenario, the final four rounds of the 32-team playoff schedule as well as the top ten bowl games and possible first round wildcard consolation bowl games would remain intact. In addition, the mentioned selection process would also apply in the expanded playoff formats. The play-in schedule and the first two rounds would be different depending on the expanded format. Let's take a look at the three possible formats in detail.

38-Team Playoff Field

Out of the three possible expanded fields, the 38-team field is the easiest to implement. Let's analyze how the potential 38-team playoff field would work.

Playoff Composition and Bracket Structure

The playoff composition and bracket structure for the 38-team playoff field would work as follows:

  • 10 Automatic Bids for Conference Champions + 28 At-Large Bids
  • The top 16 teams, nationally seeded from 1-16, plus the top ten unseeded teams would clinch the guaranteed playoff spots in the main 32-team playoff field
  • The 12 unseeded teams that gained enough consideration to be in the main 32-team playoff field must survive the Play-In Series (including unranked Conference Champions)

Play-In Games

Six play-in games would be contested on Friday and Saturday after Thanksgiving. The kickoff times would be as follows:

  • Friday after Thanksgiving Play-In Games: 12PM, 3:30PM, 7:30PM EST
  • Saturday after Thanksgiving Play-In Games: 12PM, 3:30PM, 7:30PM EST

First Round

The first round schedule would remain the same. The two regionals (likely Regionals 7 and 8) without play-in games would have all their teams play their first game on Wednesday evening/night after Thanksgiving. Two of the three Friday after Thanksgiving play-in winners would have their next game on the following Friday. The rest of the play-in winners would play on the first Saturday of December. The winners of the Wednesday first round regional games would have their next game on the following Wednesday Night and the rest of the main 32-team playoff schedule would still apply.

39-Team Playoff Field

The 39-team field is another possible expanded playoff field that can be implemented. It is of moderate difficulty to implement. Let's analyze how the potential 39-team playoff field would work.

Playoff Composition and Bracket Structure

The playoff composition and bracket structure for the 39-team playoff field would work as follows:

  • 10 Automatic Bids for Conference Champions + 29 At-Large Bids
  • The top 16 teams, nationally seeded from 1-16, plus the top nine unseeded teams would clinch the guaranteed playoff spots in the main 32-team playoff field
  • The 14 unseeded teams that gained enough consideration to be in the main 32-team playoff field must survive the Play-In Series (including unranked Conference Champions)

Play-In Games

Seven play-in games would be contested on Friday and Saturday after Thanksgiving. The kickoff times would be as follows:

  • Friday after Thanksgiving Play-In Games: 12PM, 3:30PM, 7:30PM EST
  • Saturday after Thanksgiving Play-In Games: either at 12PM, 3:30PM, 7PM and 10:30PM EST or 11AM, 2:30PM, 6PM, and 9:30PM EST

First Round

The first round schedule would be slightly different. The one regional (likely Regional 8) without play-in games would have all teams play their first round games on Wednesday evening/night after Thanksgiving. For the second regional, they can either do the following:

  • Also kickoff their first round games on Wednesday evening/night after Thanksgiving. For example, if all teams in Regional 6 play on Wednesday, the Regional 6 play-in winner would have their next game on Wednesday. The Regional 6 Play-In game would kickoff on Friday after Thanksgiving so the winner would have 5 days between games. The first round Regional 6 winners would have the regional finals on the following Wednesday evening/night.
  • Kickoff their first round games on Thursday evening/night after Thanksgiving. For example, if all teams in Regional 5 play on Thursday, the Regional 5 play-in winner would have their next game on Thursday. The Regional 5 Play-In game would kickoff on Friday after Thanksgiving so the winner would have 6 days between games. The first round Regional 5 winners would have the regional finals on the following Wednesday evening/night.

The other two Friday play-in winners would play on the following Friday, and all four Saturday play-in winners would play on the first Saturday of December. The rest of the main 32-team playoff schedule would still apply.

40-Team Playoff Field

The 40-team field is manageable, but the most difficult to implement. Let's analyze how the potential 40-team playoff field would work.

Playoff Composition and Bracket Structure

  • The 16 unseeded teams that gained enough consideration to be in the main 32-team field must survive the Play-In Series (including unranked Conference Champions)

Play-In Games

Eight play-in games would be contested on Friday and Saturday after Thanksgiving. The kickoff times would be as follows:

  • Friday after Thanksgiving Play-In Games: Either at 12PM, 3:30PM, 7PM and 10:30PM EST or 11AM, 2:30PM, 6PM, and 9:30PM EST
  • Saturday after Thanksgiving Play-In Games: Either at 12PM, 3:30PM, 7PM and 10:30PM EST or 11AM, 2:30PM, 6PM, and 9:30PM EST

First Round

The first round schedule would be different. For two of the eight regionals, they can either do the following:

  • Kickoff their first round games on Wednesday evening/night after Thanksgiving. For example, if all teams in Regionals 4 and 8 play on Wednesday, the Regional 4 and 8 play-in winners would have their next game on Wednesday. The Regional 4 and Regional 8 Play-In Games would kickoff on Friday after Thanksgiving so the winners would have 5 days between games. The first round Regional 4 and 8 winners would have the regional finals on the following Wednesday evening/night.
  • Kickoff their first round games on Thursday evening/night after Thanksgiving. For example, if all teams in Regionals 5 and 7 play on Thursday, the Regional 5 and 7 play-in winners would have their next game on Thursday. The Regional 5 and 7 Play-In Games would kickoff on Friday after Thanksgiving so the winner would have 6 days between games. The first round Regional 5 and 7 winners would have the regional finals on the following Wednesday evening/night.

The other two Friday play-in winners would play on the following Friday, and all four Saturday play-in winners would play on the first Saturday of December. The rest of the main 32-team playoff schedule would still apply.

Revenue Model

Now that we have went over the three expanded models, what would the revenue model be like? The revenue model for the final four rounds would still apply. For the first round and the play-in games, the revenue model would look like the following:

Playoff Round FBS Conferences and Notre Dame Other FBS Independents
Play-In $5M $3M
Win Play-In Game $2M Bonus $2M Bonus
Direct Entry to First Round $7M $5M

Basically, how the revenue model works for an expanded playoff field works as follows:

  • Teams in any FBS conferences plus Notre Dame that make the first round directly would earn the full $7M for their conferences, while other FBS Independents would earn the full $5M.
  • Teams in any FBS conferences plus Notre Dame that make the play-in would only earn $5M for their conferences, while other FBS Independents would only earn $3M. IF those teams win their play-in games, they would get a $2M bonus for getting into the main playoff field. This would mean that teams in any FBS conferences plus Notre Dame would eventually earn the full $7M for their conferences, while other FBS Independents would eventually earn the full $5M.

The cumulative earnings as mentioned in the revenue model would then still apply.

Upsides and Downsides

There are upsides and downsides to expanding beyond a 32-team playoff field while keeping Division I FBS intact.

Upsides

If Division I FBS remains intact, expanding beyond a 32-team playoff could work for the following reasons:

  • Paying no mind to more at-large opportunities for teams
  • More access to contend for a National Championship
  • Additional Postseason Inventory
  • Potential Revenue Increase

The reason expansion is always in discussion is to always provide more playoff spots for teams to contend for a National Championship. In addition, it provides more revenue with an expanded format.

The Downsides

Although expanding beyond a 32-team playoff would be a very cool addition, we also have the consider the downsides. Basically, the downsides are the following:

  • Selection percentages could become too high. It increased from 58.0% to up to 62.3-63.8%
  • At least 2-4 teams with losing records (at least one game below 0.500) would be selected into the secondary and tertiary playoff systems
  • At-Large Bids would dominate play-in landscape, especially if there are not many unranked conference champions

While providing additional playoff spots to play for a National Championship is always nice, the grass is sometimes not always greener on the other side.

Conclusion

Expanding the playoff field beyond 32 teams sounds like a good idea on paper. Expanding the playoff field would provide more postseason inventory for media networks. In addition, more revenue and postseason opportunities can also help Division I FBS continue to grow as a premier college football subdivision. Conferences can definitely benefit by sending more teams to the playoff field. A non-power FBS conference could gain a better chance to become a multi-bid league in an expanded playoff field.

However, we also have to consider that sometimes expanding the 32-team playoff field may not be a good idea. Although there would be more at-large opportunities for Division I FBS, the lower ranked conference champions could be pitted against each other in a play-in game given that they would be the two overall lowest ranked teams (and their conferences also become one-bid leagues). In addition, if more conference champions are ranked in the CFP Top 25, then there would not be many conference champions that would be part of the play-in landscape. The play-in landscape would be dominated by teams that earn at-large bids. In addition, the secondary and tertiary playoff systems would have less best available teams to select with more teams being selected to participate in an expanded playoff field for a National Championship.

Despite the downsides, expanding beyond a 32-team playoff field may be the right answer. Maybe the downsides are not as bad as the potential bonuses that come to expanding the 32-team playoff field. In addition, teams with losing records (for teams with one game below 0.500) that are invited to the secondary and tertiary playoff systems would have a potential chance to finish their seasons strong with a winning record. Whether the playoff expands to 38, 39, or 40 teams, the upsides can definitely overcome the downsides to expanding beyond 32 teams.
