Revenue Distribution

DISCLAIMER: This section is only my judgement of the potential in how much a 32-team field can potentially make for all of Division I FBS. The reality can become different from the predictions.

The current College Football Playoff format still distributes revenue for all the bowl games from Mid-December to early January. Since the bowl games are still involved as consolation games and/or playoff hosts, that can serve as potential additional revenue for any highly ranked/seeded playoff participants. Otherwise, the 32-team playoff could still earn more potential revenue than any format below 32 teams. The payout of the bowl games would be a bit different than the current payouts of the College Football Playoff. Teams would receive the following payout for their success in the playoffs:

Second Round $9M $7.5M

The payout distribution for each of the New Year's Six Bowl Games depends on which ones would be hosting the quarterfinals as well as the semifinals. LEt's take a look at each of the payouts that could happen.

Cumulative Earnings

The cumulative earning a team can earn for their respective conferences or as Notre Dame or as an FBS Independent would be the following:

Playoff Participation FBS Conferences and Notre Dame Other FBS Independents
First Round $7M $5M
Second Round $16M $12.5M
Quarterfinals $28M $22.5M
Semifinals $42M $33.5M
National Championship $60M $48.5M
The total distribution (without a non-Notre Dame Independent) for each round would be the following:
Playoff Round Number of Teams Payout Per Team Total Distributed
First Round 32 $7M $224M
Second Round 16 $9M $144M
Quarterfinals 8 $12M $96M
Semifinals 4 $14M $56M
National Championship 2 $18M $36M
The revenue distribution would work as follows:
  • Conferences accumulate all earnings from their teams' performance
  • Each conference distributes according to their own media deal agreements
  • Example: A conference that has 6 teams advancing to the second round would earn $54M for that round alone
Here are the advantages:
  • Performance-based: Rewards competitive success
  • Scalable: Deep playoff runs = exponentially higher earnings
  • Conference incentives: More bids + advancement = more conference revenue
  • Maintains competitive balance: non-power FBS auto-bids ensure access; power conference depth earns at-large majority
